Ontology for Risk Estimation in Construction (OCQA-RISK)

The OCQA-Risk ontology extends OCQA to model potential non-conformities and estimate non-conformity costs (NCCs) (see Figure 1). The classes, relations, and properties of OCQA-Risk shown in Figure 2 are essential for the estimation of NCC. The OCQA-Risk ontology models causal links between construction activities and defects using ocqa-risk:PotentialNonConfirmity. Historical defect data drive the ocqa-risk:Probability values, while remedy costs are stored in ocqa-risk:AverageNCC and tied to component production costs via ocqa-risk:hasProductionCosts. A SHACL rule then multiplies probability by average cost to infer the expected non-conformity cost (ocqa-risk:hasPotentialNCC). Finally, ocqa-risk:detects connects each inspection (a subclass of dicp:Activity) to the potential defects it can identify. A summary of relevenat data properties to store estiamted values is listed below:

  • ocqa-risk:hasProductionCosts stores the component‚Äôs production cost.
  • ocqa-risk:AverageNCC describes the imminent NCCs
  • ocqa-risk:hasPotentialNCC is the risk resulting by multiplying the risk:hasAverageNCC with ocqa-risk:Probability

Risk Estimation Methods

The achievement of the set quality objectives is the result of internal and external factors. ISO 31000 describes the "effect of uncertainty on objectives" as a risk. Applied to quality manage- ment (QM), the "non-fulfilment of a requirement" (nonconformity, also known as a defect) rep- resents a quality risk. If the intended or specified use is not pos- sible as a result of the quality deviation, it is a defect. The risk consequences of defective work are NCC from rework, repair, scrapping, disposal, handling of rejects, un- planned sorting tests, repeat tests, downtime, warranties, and product liability. In addition to economic damage, errors and defects in the AEC sector disrupt construction processes, waste environmental resources, and damage the company's image.

The estimation of NCC in AEC uses a for quantitative risk assessment, which is illustrated in following formular. probability of occurrence describes on which probability a Failure will happen. This probability value will be based on historical defect data. Consequence is approximated by the expected non-conformity cost.

Risk = Probability of occurrence √ó Consequence

In this work the non-confirmity cost is limited to the component and no furhter costs are considered. Nonconformity costs (NCCs) arise when products or services fail to meet defined quality specifications. Nonconformity costs can be divided into direct costs ‚Äî those incurred to legally correct defects ‚Äî and indirect costs‚Äîthose resulting from the broader impact of defects (see Table 1).

SHACL rule Potential Non-Conformity Cost inferencing

In OCQA-Risk, potential non-conformity costs (NCCs) are inferred entirely within the ontology by a SHACL-SPARQL rule (See Figure 3 and Listing 1): the rule targets every ocqa:Characteristic, filters those linked to screed flatness and ocqa-screed:Screed, and then CONSTRUCTs a new ocqa-risk:Uneveness instance of type ocqa-risk:PotentialNonConfirmity. This instance is attached to its inspection via ocqa-risk:detects. Next, the rule retrieves the component‚Äôs production cost and the characteristic‚Äôs probability, computes the average remedy cost from the cost catalog, and binds two values: ?averageNCC and ?PotentialNCC (the product of ?averageNCC and ?probability). The resulting ocqa-risk:hasPotentialNCC literal is then asserted on each defect instance, making NCC estimation seamless, transparent, and fully declarative.

Figure 3:NCC estimation using SHACL rules in OCQA-RISK

To validate OCQA-Risk, we used a screed installation project (DERICHS & KONERTZ, DEKO) as a case study. Instance data were imported into GraphDB and mapped to OCQA to deviate of potential non-conformities and related non-conformity costs via SHACL rules. The rules 


# Listing 1: SHACL Statements to Infer Potential Non-Conformity and NCC for Screed
ocqa-screed:NonConfirmityShapeScreed
    a sh:NodeShape;
    sh:targetClass ocqa:Characteristic;
    sh:rule [
        a sh:SPARQLRule;
        sh:condition [
            sh:property [
                sh:path (sh:inversePath ocqa-screed:hasFlatness);
                sh:class ocqa-screed:Screed
            ];
        ];
        sh:construct """
        [Prefixes]
        CONSTRUCT {
            $this ocqa-risk:hasPotentialNonConfirmity ?PotentialNonConfirmity.
            ?PotentialNonConfirmity a ocqa-risk:Uneveness;
                                     a ocqa-risk:PotentialNonConfirmity.
            ?Inspection ocqa-risk:detects ?PotentialNonConfirmity.
            ?PotentialNonConfirmity ocqa-risk:AverageNCC ?averageNCC.
            ?PotentialNonConfirmity ocqa-risk:hasPotentialNCC ?PotentialNCC.
        }
        WHERE {
            $this ^ocqa:hasInspectionCharacteristic ?Inspection;
                  ^ocqa-screed:hasFlatness/ocqa-risk:hasProductionCost ?ProductionCostScreed.
            ?Inspection ocqa-risk:probability ?probability.
            
            BIND ((?ProductionCostScreed * 0.1) AS ?averageNCC).
            BIND (?averageNCC * ?probability AS ?PotentialNCC).
        }""";
    ];

This declarative, standards‚Äêbased approach enables domain experts to maintain and adapt cost estimation for potentail non-conformaty costs in construction using a logic directly in the ontology, without external scripts based on semantic web standards.

The SHACL shapes for OCQA-RISK are published at https://SebSeis.github.io/OCQA/Modules/RISK/InferenceRule_PotentialNCC.ttl and can be validated against your RDF with the pySHACL runner script available at https://SebSeis.github.io/OCQA/Modules/RISK/run_shacl_ac_inference.py (e.g., pyshacl --shacl shapes/ocqa-ac-shapes.ttl --data data/ocqa-ac-data.ttl --output results/ocqa-ac-results.ttl). The python script is imrpoved and commented using ChatGPT.
